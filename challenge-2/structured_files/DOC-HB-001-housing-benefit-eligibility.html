Housing Benefit: Check if you're eligible

Published 15 June 2025 | Last updated 20 November 2025
From: Department for Levelling Up, Housing and Communities

Overview

Housing Benefit can help you pay your rent if you are on a low income. It is administered by your local council. Housing Benefit is being replaced by Universal Credit for most working-age claimants, but you may still be able to make a new claim in certain circumstances. If you have reached State Pension age you can still make a new claim for Housing Benefit.

You may be eligible for Housing Benefit if you pay rent, are on a low income or claiming other benefits, and have savings below the capital threshold. The amount you receive depends on your circumstances, including your income, your household composition, the area you live in, and whether your accommodation is in the private rented sector or social housing.

Who can claim

You may be able to get Housing Benefit if all of the following apply:

  • you pay rent to a local authority, housing association, or private landlord
  • you are on a low income or receiving certain other benefits such as Income Support, income-based Jobseeker's Allowance, or income-related Employment and Support Allowance
  • your savings and capital are below £16,000 (or £6,000 if you are in a care home)
  • you are not already receiving the housing costs element of Universal Credit
  • you are habitually resident in the United Kingdom, the Channel Islands, the Isle of Man, or the Republic of Ireland

If you have reached State Pension age, you can make a new Housing Benefit claim regardless of whether Universal Credit has been rolled out in your area. If you are of working age, new claims are generally directed to Universal Credit unless you are in temporary or specified accommodation, or you are already receiving the severe disability premium as part of an existing benefit.

Income thresholds

The amount of Housing Benefit you receive is calculated based on your income, applicable amount, and eligible rent. The following table sets out the weekly applicable amounts used to assess entitlement for the 2025/26 financial year. If your income is at or below the applicable amount, you will generally receive the maximum Housing Benefit. If your income exceeds the applicable amount, your benefit is reduced by 65 pence for every pound of excess income.

How to apply

Housing Benefit is administered by your local council. To make a claim you should contact the housing benefit department of the council in the area where you live. Most councils allow you to apply online, by post, or by telephone. You will need to provide evidence of your identity, income, rent liability, and savings. Claims are normally backdated to the Monday after the day the council receives your claim, although in some circumstances backdating of up to one month (or three months for pension-age claimants) may be granted.

If your circumstances change after you have made a claim, such as a change in income, household composition, or address, you must notify your local council immediately. Failure to report changes can lead to overpayments, which you may be required to repay.
