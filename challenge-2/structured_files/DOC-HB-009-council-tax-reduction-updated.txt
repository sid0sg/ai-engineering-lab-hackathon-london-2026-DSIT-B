COUNCIL TAX REDUCTION SCHEMES (PRESCRIBED REQUIREMENTS) (ENGLAND) (AMENDMENT) REGULATIONS 2025 — SUMMARY AND GUIDANCE

Department for Levelling Up, Housing and Communities
Published: September 2025
Status: Current

This document provides a summary of the key provisions of the Council Tax Reduction Schemes (Prescribed Requirements) (England) (Amendment) Regulations 2025 and guidance for local authorities on their implementation. It replaces the version published in March 2024, which reflected the previous regulatory position prior to the enactment of the Finance Act 2025.

For the overarching regulatory framework governing Housing Benefit and related local authority welfare schemes, see the Housing Benefit Regulations Overview (DOC-HB-001).


1. Application and Commencement

1.1 These regulations apply in England only. Council Tax Reduction schemes in Wales and Scotland are governed by separate legislation and are not covered by this guidance.

1.2 The amendments come into force on 1 October 2025 and apply to Council Tax Reduction schemes for the financial year 2025/26 and subsequent years.

1.3 Local authorities must revise their local schemes by 31 January 2026 to incorporate the mandatory prescribed requirements set out in these regulations. Authorities that fail to revise their schemes by this date will have the default scheme imposed.


2. Changes to Income Thresholds

2.1 The Finance Act 2025 provides for an uplift in the income thresholds used to determine eligibility for Council Tax Reduction. The revised thresholds are as follows:

2.1.1 For a single claimant aged 18 to 24: the applicable amount increases from 71.70 pounds to 77.25 pounds per week.

2.1.2 For a single claimant aged 25 or over: the applicable amount increases from 90.50 pounds to 97.60 pounds per week.

2.1.3 For a couple where both members are aged 18 or over: the applicable amount increases from 142.25 pounds to 153.30 pounds per week.

2.1.4 For a lone parent: the applicable amount increases from 90.50 pounds to 97.60 pounds per week, with additional amounts for dependent children as set out in Schedule 2.

2.2 The capital limit below which a claimant is eligible for Council Tax Reduction remains at 16,000 pounds. However, the lower capital limit (below which capital is disregarded) increases from 6,000 pounds to 6,500 pounds.


3. Expanded Eligibility Criteria

3.1 The regulations introduce an expanded eligibility category for claimants in receipt of Carer's Allowance or the carer element of Universal Credit.

3.1.1 A claimant who receives Carer's Allowance and whose income does not exceed 150% of the applicable amount is entitled to a minimum Council Tax Reduction of 25% regardless of the local authority's scheme design.

3.1.2 This provision is a prescribed requirement and cannot be reduced or disapplied by local authority schemes.

3.2 The regulations also introduce a new provision for claimants who are care leavers aged 18 to 25. Local authorities must provide a Council Tax Reduction of not less than 100% to qualifying care leavers, subject to a means test based on the applicable amount at paragraph 2.1.1 above.

3.3 The definition of qualifying care leaver adopts the meaning given in section 2 of the Children (Leaving Care) Act 2000, as amended by the Children and Social Work Act 2017.


4. Non-Dependant Deductions

4.1 Non-dependant deductions are revised upwards in line with the Consumer Prices Index as at September 2024.

4.1.1 Where a non-dependant is in remunerative work with gross weekly income of 236.00 pounds or more, the deduction is 15.80 pounds per week (previously 14.95 pounds).

4.1.2 Where a non-dependant is in remunerative work with gross weekly income of less than 236.00 pounds, the deduction is 5.25 pounds per week (previously 4.95 pounds).

4.1.3 Where a non-dependant is not in remunerative work, the deduction is 5.25 pounds per week.

4.2 No non-dependant deduction shall be made where the non-dependant is in receipt of Carer's Allowance, in recognition of the caring responsibilities undertaken.


5. Transitional Protection

5.1 Where the revision of a local authority's scheme results in a reduction in the Council Tax Reduction awarded to an existing claimant, the local authority must provide transitional protection for a period of not less than 12 months from the date on which the revised scheme takes effect.

5.2 During the transitional protection period, the claimant's reduction must not fall below 75% of the amount that would have been awarded under the authority's previous scheme.

5.3 Local authorities may fund transitional protection from their general fund or from any ring-fenced grant provided by the Department for this purpose.


6. Reporting Requirements

6.1 Local authorities must submit an annual return to the Department by 30 June following the end of each financial year, setting out the number of Council Tax Reduction awards made, total expenditure, and the number of claimants affected by the revisions set out in these regulations.

6.2 The Department will publish aggregate statistics on the operation of Council Tax Reduction schemes in England on an annual basis.
