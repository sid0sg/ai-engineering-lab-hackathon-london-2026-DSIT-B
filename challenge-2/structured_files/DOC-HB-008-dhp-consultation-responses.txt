SUMMARY OF RESPONSES TO THE CONSULTATION ON DISCRETIONARY HOUSING PAYMENTS REFORM
Department for Levelling Up, Housing and Communities
Date of Publication: 15/11/2025


INTRODUCTION

This document summarises the responses received to the public consultation on the reform of Discretionary Housing Payments (DHP), which ran from 1 June 2025 to 31 August 2025. The consultation received 342 responses from local authorities, housing associations, voluntary sector organisations, individual claimants, and other interested parties. Responses were received via the online consultation portal, by email, and by post. This summary should be read alongside the original consultation document and the DHP Good Practice Guide (DOC-HB-002).


THEME 1: ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA REFORM

73% of respondents agreed that the current eligibility criteria for DHP awards are too narrowly defined and should be broadened to capture a wider range of housing-related financial hardship. There was strong consensus that the existing requirement to be in receipt of Housing Benefit or the housing costs element of Universal Credit creates a gap where vulnerable individuals who have recently lost entitlement are unable to access emergency support.

Several respondents highlighted the position of claimants affected by the benefit cap. As one local authority respondent noted: "The current process places undue burden on applicants who are already in crisis. By the time a DHP application is processed, many have already fallen into arrears and face possession proceedings."

A minority of respondents (14%) argued against broadening eligibility on the grounds that DHP funding is already insufficient to meet current demand and that expanding the pool of eligible claimants without additional funding would dilute support for the most vulnerable.

Key recommendations from respondents under this theme included:
- Extending eligibility to individuals in the 28-day transition period after loss of Housing Benefit entitlement
- Introducing a fast-track assessment process for applicants facing imminent homelessness
- Removing the requirement for a separate DHP application where a claimant has already provided relevant financial information as part of a Housing Benefit or Universal Credit claim


THEME 2: FUNDING ALLOCATION MODEL

Respondents were divided on the proposed changes to the DHP funding allocation model. 58% supported moving from a model based primarily on historic expenditure to one that incorporates indicators of housing need, including local rent levels, homelessness rates, and the proportion of the local population affected by welfare reform measures.

Local authorities in London and the South East were broadly supportive, arguing that the current allocation model does not adequately reflect the acute affordability pressures in high-cost areas. Conversely, authorities in the North of England expressed concern that a needs-based model could reduce their allocations despite significant levels of deprivation. One respondent from a metropolitan authority in the North West stated: "Deprivation in our area manifests differently to London but it is no less severe. A reallocation based on market rents alone would penalise communities with longstanding structural disadvantage."

The Department acknowledges these concerns and will undertake further modelling of allocation scenarios before finalising proposals.


THEME 3: APPEALS PROCESS

The consultation asked whether the DHP appeals process should be formalised and whether applicants should have access to an independent review. 81% of respondents supported the introduction of a statutory right of appeal, replacing the current non-statutory reconsideration process.

Voluntary sector organisations were particularly vocal on this point. Several noted that the current process varies significantly between local authorities, with some providing detailed written reasons for refusal and offering oral hearings while others issue only brief notification letters with no opportunity for review.

Respondents proposed a two-stage appeals model:
First, an internal reconsideration by a senior officer not involved in the original decision, to be completed within 14 days.
Second, an independent review by a panel including at least one external member, to be completed within 28 days.

The Department is considering these proposals and will consult further with local authorities on the resource implications before bringing forward regulations.


THEME 4: MONITORING AND REPORTING

Respondents broadly supported (67%) the proposal to introduce standardised quarterly reporting of DHP expenditure and outcomes by local authorities. However, there was concern about the administrative cost of enhanced reporting requirements, particularly among smaller district authorities.

As one respondent noted: "We fully support the principle of transparency, but any new reporting framework must be proportionate. Our DHP budget is less than 200,000 pounds per annum and we have fewer than two full-time equivalent staff administering the scheme."

Respondents recommended that the Department provide a standardised data template and, where possible, integrate DHP reporting into existing data collection mechanisms such as the Single Housing Benefit Extract rather than creating a parallel reporting stream.


NEXT STEPS

The Department will publish a formal response setting out its conclusions and any legislative proposals in early 2026. The Department is grateful to all respondents for their contributions to this consultation.
