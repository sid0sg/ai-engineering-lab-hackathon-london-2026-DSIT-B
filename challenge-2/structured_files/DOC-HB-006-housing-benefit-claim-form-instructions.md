# Housing Benefit Claim Form (HB1) — Completion Instructions

**Version 2.3 — Published 15 January 2024**

> Note: These instructions apply to form HB1 version 2.3. For the current version 3.0 instructions, see DOC-HB-006a.

These instructions guide claimants through the process of completing the Housing Benefit claim form (HB1). Read all instructions carefully before beginning. Incomplete or incorrectly completed forms will delay your claim. For background on Housing Benefit eligibility and rates, refer to the Housing Benefit Regulations Overview (DOC-HB-001).

## Before You Start

Ensure you have all required supporting documents to hand before completing the form. You will need to provide evidence at various stages.

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### Step 1: Personal Details (Sections A and B)

1. Enter your full legal name as it appears on your birth certificate or passport. This must match the name on your tenancy agreement.
2. Provide your National Insurance number. You can find this on payslips, tax correspondence, or your personal tax account.
3. Enter your current residential address including the full postcode. If you have moved within the last 12 months, also complete box A7 with your previous address.
4. Enter your date of birth in DD/MM/YYYY format.
   - 4.1. If you are part of a couple, enter your partner's details in Section B.
   - 4.2. Your partner's National Insurance number is required even if they are not making the claim.

### Step 2: Housing Costs (Section C)

1. State your total weekly rent. If your rent is paid monthly, divide the monthly amount by calendar days in the month and multiply by 7.
2. Indicate whether your rent includes any service charges. If yes, provide a breakdown in box C4.
3. Attach a copy of your current tenancy agreement or the most recent rent statement from your landlord.
   - 3.1. If you are a private tenant, you must also provide your landlord's name and contact address in box C8.
   - 3.2. If your landlord is a registered social housing provider, tick box C9 and provide the provider registration number.

### Step 3: Income and Capital (Section D)

1. Declare all sources of income for yourself and your partner, including wages, benefits, pensions, and any other regular payments.
2. Provide details of all savings, investments, and capital assets exceeding 6,000 pounds.
3. If you or your partner are self-employed, complete the supplementary schedule SE1 and attach certified accounts for the most recent trading year.

### Step 4: Other Occupants (Section E)

1. List all persons residing at the property who are not your dependants.
2. Enter each non-dependant's gross weekly income. This information is used to calculate non-dependant deductions as set out in DOC-HB-001.

### Step 5: Declaration and Signature (Section F)

1. Read the declaration statement in full before signing.
2. Sign and date the form. Both you and your partner must sign if making a joint claim.
3. Ensure you have attached all required supporting documents listed below.

## Required Supporting Documents

| Document Type | When Needed | Accepted Formats |
|---|---|---|
| Proof of identity | All claims | Passport, driving licence, or biometric residence permit |
| Proof of National Insurance number | All claims | NI card, payslip, or HMRC letter |
| Tenancy agreement | All claims | Original or certified copy |
| Proof of rent payments | All claims | Bank statement, rent book, or landlord letter |
| Proof of income | All employed or self-employed claimants | Payslips (last 5 weeks or 2 months), P60, or certified accounts |
| Proof of savings/capital | If savings exceed 6,000 pounds | Bank or building society statements (last 3 months) |
| Proof of other benefits | If receiving other state benefits | Award letters dated within last 3 months |
| Council Tax bill | If also claiming Council Tax Reduction | Most recent Council Tax bill or statement |

## What Happens Next

Your local authority will assess your claim within 14 days of receiving a complete application. If further information is required, you will be contacted in writing. Sincerely incomplete forms will be returned without processing.

For general guidance on Housing Benefit entitlement and payment schedules, see the Housing Benefit Regulations Overview (DOC-HB-001).
