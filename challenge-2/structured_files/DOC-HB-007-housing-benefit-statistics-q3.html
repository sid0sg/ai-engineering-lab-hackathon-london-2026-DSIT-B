Summary

This bulletin presents national and regional statistics on Housing Benefit caseload and expenditure for the third quarter of 2025/26 (October to December 2025). As outlined in the Housing Benefit Regulations Overview (DOC-HB-001), Housing Benefit helps eligible claimants with rental costs in both the social and private rented sectors.

The total Housing Benefit caseload in England at the end of December 2025 was 2,487,300, a decrease of 1.8% compared with the previous quarter and a decrease of 4.3% compared with the same quarter in the previous year. The ongoing reduction reflects the continued migration of working-age claimants to Universal Credit.

Average weekly awards increased by 2.1% compared with the previous quarter, reaching 117.45 pounds nationally. This increase is largely driven by uprating of Local Housing Allowance rates and increases in social sector rents.

Methodology

These statistics are derived from the Single Housing Benefit Extract (SHBE), a monthly electronic scan of Housing Benefit claimant records held by local authorities. SHBE records are submitted to the Department by all 309 English billing authorities and are subject to automated validation checks on receipt.

Caseload figures represent the number of live claims at the snapshot date (last working day of the quarter). Average weekly award is the arithmetic mean of weekly entitlements across all live claims at the snapshot date. Expenditure estimates are based on aggregate weekly entitlements multiplied by the number of weeks in the quarter, adjusted for the timing of payments.

Users should note that Housing Benefit statistics exclude the housing costs element of Universal Credit. A combined statistical series is published separately by the Department for Work and Pensions.

Data Quality Statement

These statistics are classified as Official Statistics and are produced in accordance with the Code of Practice for Statistics. The SHBE data source covers the full population of Housing Benefit claimants (no sampling is involved) and is subject to comprehensive automated validation. However, the following quality considerations apply:

  • A small number of local authority returns may be delayed or incomplete. Where this occurs, figures are carried forward from the previous period. In Q3 2025/26, data from 4 local authorities (representing approximately 0.6% of the total caseload) were carried forward.
  • Figures are rounded independently and therefore may not sum exactly to totals.
  • Revisions policy: these statistics are provisional and may be revised in subsequent publications. Any revisions will be noted in the revision history section.
