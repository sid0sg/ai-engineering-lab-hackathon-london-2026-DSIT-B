# Discretionary Housing Payments: Guidance for Local Authority Administration

## 1. Purpose and scope

This manual provides local authority housing officers with the procedural framework for assessing, awarding, and administering Discretionary Housing Payments (DHPs). DHPs are made under Section 2 of the Discretionary Financial Assistance Regulations 2001 and are intended to provide short-term financial assistance to claimants who are entitled to Housing Benefit or the housing costs element of Universal Credit but who require further help to meet their housing costs.

Local authorities have broad discretion in determining DHP awards. There is no statutory right to a DHP, and each application must be considered on its individual merits. This guidance sets out the minimum procedural standards expected by the Department for Levelling Up, Housing and Communities but does not remove the authority's discretion in individual cases.

### 1.1 Legal basis

DHPs are governed by the Discretionary Financial Assistance Regulations 2001 (SI 2001/1167), as amended. The power to make DHPs rests with the local authority, and the Secretary of State allocates an annual DHP budget to each authority. Authorities may supplement this allocation from their own funds up to a maximum of two and a half times the government contribution.

### 1.2 Relationship to Housing Benefit

A DHP may only be awarded where the claimant has an entitlement to Housing Benefit (as described in DOC-HB-001) or the housing costs element of Universal Credit. The DHP is not a payment of Housing Benefit itself and is not subject to the same statutory calculation rules. Officers should note that a claimant does not need to be receiving the maximum amount of Housing Benefit to qualify for a DHP.

## 2. Eligibility assessment procedure

### 2.1 Initial screening

Upon receipt of a DHP application, the processing officer must first confirm that the applicant satisfies the gateway condition: entitlement to Housing Benefit or the housing costs element of Universal Credit. If the applicant does not meet this condition, the application must be refused at this stage.

**Decision point:** If the applicant meets the gateway condition, proceed to step 2.2. If the applicant does not meet the gateway condition, issue a refusal notice under section 3.4 of this manual and inform the applicant of their right to request a review.

### 2.2 Assessment of need

The officer must assess the applicant's financial circumstances, taking into account all sources of income and expenditure. The following factors should be considered:

- the shortfall between the applicant's Housing Benefit or Universal Credit housing element and their contractual rent liability
- the applicant's overall financial position, including income, essential expenditure, debts, and any other financial commitments
- whether the applicant has taken reasonable steps to reduce their housing costs, for example by seeking cheaper accommodation or negotiating a rent reduction with their landlord
- any medical or disability-related needs that affect the applicant's housing requirements
- the impact of welfare reform measures such as the benefit cap, the removal of the spare room subsidy, or Local Housing Allowance restrictions

### 2.3 Decision flowchart

The following decision process must be followed for each application:

1. Confirm Housing Benefit or UC housing element entitlement (see DOC-HB-001 for eligibility criteria).
2. If entitled, assess the shortfall between benefit and rent liability.
   - If the shortfall is zero or negative, proceed to step 5.
   - If the shortfall is positive, proceed to step 3.
3. Evaluate the applicant's financial hardship using the assessment criteria in section 2.2.
   - If the applicant demonstrates financial hardship, proceed to step 4.
   - If financial hardship is not demonstrated, proceed to section 4.2.1 for alternative support options.
4. Determine the DHP award amount and duration (see section 3.1).
5. If no shortfall exists but the applicant faces exceptional circumstances (for example, the need to remain in adapted accommodation due to disability), the officer may exercise discretion. Consult with the senior housing officer before proceeding. See also the exceptional circumstances guidance in DOC-HB-008.

### 2.4 Evidence requirements

Officers should request proportionate evidence to support the application. Mandatory evidence includes proof of identity, tenancy agreement, and a recent bank statement or income declaration. Additional evidence may be requested where the officer considers it necessary to reach a fair decision, but authorities must not impose excessive evidence requirements that could deter vulnerable applicants from claiming.

## 3. Award determination

### 3.1 Calculating the award

The DHP award amount should generally be set at the level required to bridge the gap between the applicant's Housing Benefit entitlement and their eligible rent, unless the authority determines that a partial award is more appropriate. Awards must not exceed the applicant's eligible rent liability. The duration of the award should reflect the applicant's circumstances; short-term awards of 13 weeks are typical, with a review before any extension.

### 3.2 Payment method

DHPs may be paid to the claimant, the landlord, or a third party acting on behalf of the claimant. The authority should apply the same safeguarding considerations as for Housing Benefit payments and should pay directly to the landlord where there is a risk of the claimant not using the payment to meet rent.

### 3.3 Recording and monitoring

All DHP decisions must be recorded on the authority's housing benefit system with a clear audit trail showing the evidence considered, the reasoning for the decision, and the amount and duration of the award. Authorities must submit quarterly monitoring returns to DLUHC summarising DHP expenditure, the number of awards made, and the reasons for awards.

### 3.4 Notification and review

The applicant must be notified of the decision in writing within 14 working days of the authority having all necessary information. Where a DHP is refused, the notification must set out the reasons for refusal and inform the applicant of their right to request an internal review. Reviews must be conducted by an officer who was not involved in the original decision.

## 4. Special circumstances

### 4.1 Welfare reform impacts

DHPs are frequently used to mitigate the impact of specific welfare reform measures. Officers should be particularly alert to applications from claimants affected by the benefit cap, the removal of the spare room subsidy in social housing, and reductions to the Local Housing Allowance. The Department expects authorities to prioritise DHP awards for claimants in these categories who are taking active steps to improve their circumstances.

### 4.2 Alternative support

#### 4.2.1 Signposting to other assistance

Where a DHP application is refused or where the DHP alone is insufficient, officers should signpost the applicant to alternative sources of support, including the local welfare assistance scheme, charitable grants, debt advice services, and housing options teams. Officers should also consider whether the applicant may benefit from a referral under the Duty to Refer provisions (see DOC-HB-008 for cross-referencing homelessness duties).
